2.6

Objects in orbit around Earth follow a uniform and repeating trajectory around the planet. The speed of this orbit depends on the distance from the object to the center of the Earth. Often, artifical satellites maintain a consistent distance from the Earth, forming a circular orbit. Then, at any time, the distance from the object to the center of the Earth is the radius of that circle. In this case, the orbital velocity can be computed with the following equation:

\( \sqrt{G * m_E \over r} \)

where:
\(G\) = the universal gravitational constant = \(6.673 * 10^{11} {N\cdot m^2/kg^2}\)
\(m_E\) = the mass of Earth = \(5.98 * 10^{24} kg\)
\(r\) = the distance from the object to the center of the Earth

The radius of the Earth is \(6.38 * 10^6 m\).

Write a program that prompts the user for a satellite's distance from the Earth's crust. Assume the satellite follows the circular orbit described above. Compute its orbital velocity and display the result to the user.
Hint: Use the Math.pow() method or the Math.sqrt() method.
