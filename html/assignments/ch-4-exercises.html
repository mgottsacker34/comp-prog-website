4.101: Multiple addition quizzes

Write a program that gives the user up to four addition quizzes. Each quiz should generate two integers between 10 and 20, and prompt the user to answer the sum of them. After each of the first three quizzes, ask the user whether they want to continue. After the final quiz (or after they stop answering questions), print the user's score as a percentage with two decimal places.

Sample runs: 


What is 13 + 15? 28
That is correct. Would you like another quiz? (y/n): y
What is 11 + 20? 30
That is incorrect. Would you like another quiz? (y/n): y
What is 13 + 16? 29 
That is correct. Would you like to continue? (y/n): n
Your score is 66.67%
                

What is 14 + 11? 25
That is correct. Would you like another quiz? (y/n): y
What is 11 + 11? 22
That is correct. Would you like another quiz? (y/n): y
What is 13 + 14? 27 
That is correct. Would you like to continue? (y/n): y
What is 20 + 10? 30
That is correct.
Your score is 100.00%


4.102: What are the odds?

Write a program that plays the "odds game." Prompt the user to enter a task for which they want to play the game. Then, prompt the user to enter the highest bounds of the odds game. Generate a random number between 1 and that upper bound. Prompt the user to enter the number they are guessing, and tell them whether they guessed the number.

Sample runs: 


What are the odds...

What task are you putting on the line? eat a tablespoon of cinnamon
What are the odds you will eat a tablespoon of cinnamon? 1 in 20
Enter your number between 1 and 20: 6
The computer generated 6 as well. You must eat a tablespoon of cinnamon!
                

What are the odds...

What task are you putting on the line? drink a gallon of milk
What are the odds you will drink a gallon of milk? 1 in 50
Enter your number between 1 and 20: 26
The computer generated 15. You do not have to drink a gallon of milk.


4.103: Password verifier

Write a program that prompts the user to enter an 6-character plaintext password that contains at least one of the following:

  • uppercase letter (although the first character cannot be uppercase)
  • lowercase letter
  • number
  • special symbol (a secondary key from the top row of the keyboard that you get from pressing Shift + [number])
Notify the user about whether the password meets the requirements, and improvements they can make.

Sample runs: 


This program verifies that your 6-character plaintext password contains at least one of each of the following:
    - uppercase letter (although the first character cannot be uppercase)
    - lowercase letter
    - number 
    - special symbol (such as ! or @ or #)
Enter your password: sAfe!1
That password meets our strength requirements.

4.104: Password strengthener

Write a program that prompts the user to enter an 6-character plaintext password. Transform the password to make it stronger. Specifically:

  • Capitalize the second-last character if it is a letter.
  • Replace all occurrences of the following characters with their leet speak alternatives.
character leet
a or A @
e or E 3
s or S $

Sample runs: 


Enter an 6-character plaintext password: secure
Your strengthened password is: $3cuR3
                

Enter an 6-character plaintext password: longPassword
Your password is too long to be processed.
